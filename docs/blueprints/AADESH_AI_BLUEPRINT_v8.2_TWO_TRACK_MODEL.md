## TRACK 1: INDIVIDUAL MODEL (Phase 0-2)

### How It Works
1. Officer hears about Aadesh AI from Banu or another officer
2. Goes to aadesh-ai.in
3. Creates account (Google login or email)
4. Gets 2-3 FREE demo orders to try
5. Likes it → buys credits (Rs 500/order or monthly pack)
6. Pays via UPI / card / Razorpay
7. Uses it for personal drafting work
8. Tells colleagues → they sign up

### Why This Is Legal
- Officer is NOT buying software for the office
- Officer is using a PERSONAL TOOL to help with drafting
- Same as paying a drafter from pocket (Rs 1,000-2,000)
- Same as paying for ChatGPT subscription (Rs 1,650/month)
- The OUTPUT (order) is still reviewed, edited, signed by the officer
- AI is a drafting ASSISTANT, not a decision-maker
- No government data is stored permanently — officer inputs case details, gets draft, done

## TRACK 2: DEPARTMENT MODEL (Phase 3+)

### How It Activates (Not forced — happens naturally)
When 50-100 officers are individually paying for Aadesh AI:
- A DC or Revenue Secretary notices the pattern
- Officers request: "Can the department pay for this?"
- Department evaluates → formal procurement
- Aadesh AI is already proven, already has user testimonials
- Procurement becomes easy because demand already exists

### Why Track 2 Needs Track 1 First
| Without Track 1 | With Track 1 |
|----------------|-------------|
| "What is this? Never heard of it." | "50 officers already use it daily." |
| "Show me proof it works." | "Here are 1,000 orders generated." |
| "Why should we trust AI?" | "Banu scored 100/100 on quality." |
| "We need a tender process." | "Officers are paying from pocket — let's formalize." |

### Track 2 Pricing (when department buys)
